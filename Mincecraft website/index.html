Minecraft - a quick intro

For those of you that have come to this site without knowing what minecraft is (for whatever reason that may be...) Minecraft is an awesome sandbox style game where you can select to play in one of several different gamemodes: SURVIVAL MODE; CREATIVE MODE; ADVENTURE MODE; SPECTATOR MODE; HARDCORE MODE. This sandbox game is one where, in these gamemodes, you build, craft, and survive. you can do so much in this game, the only limit is your imagination.

Gamemodes

SURVIVAL MODE

In this mode, your objective is to survive. You will spawn in your world (in a randmly decided spawn point), and you must immediately start to gather and craft items so you can create a shelter (either by placing blocks, or carving out a hollow in a mountain or the ground). The objective is normally to have your shelter built before the first night, because your shelter is the main thing protecting you from the monsters that spawn in the night (i.e. zombes, creepers). When you die, and you will in he game, you respawn at your spawn point, which has a changeable location. to show your health level (determines what it will take to kill you in your state) there is a health bar. There is also a hunger bar. At 9-10 points, you can regen health, any lower and you cannot. There is an oxygen bar as well if you are under water. When the oxygen bar runs out, you start to lose health. When you come up, you have to wait for a bit for your Oxygen to regenerate. There are also difficulty levels in the gamemodes: peaceful, easy, normal, and hard. These can be changed in-game, and they determine how many monsters spawn at night, how much damage they do, what equipement they will spawn with (if any) and also how much damage you will take if your hunger bar runs out. there are also neutral and friendly creatures, some of which who could attack you, but only in certain conditions. For example, Chickens and Villagers are passive, but Wolves will attack you if you attack them, unless you tame them with a bone. More detail


CREATIVE MODE

Basically survival mode, but you have can't get hurt and no hunger or oxygen bar. you also have access to every item in the game, including ones that you are normally unable to get in survival mode, like spawn eggs, which are eggs that when used spawn a specific creature. For example, a wolf spawn egg will spawn in a wolf. Although chickens do lay eggs, these have a possibility of spawining in a chicken. Chicken spawn eggs will guarantee a spawned chicken. You also have acces to the ability to fly, and if cheats are on in that world, you can switch gamemodes, as well as control the entire world, with changing the weather and time of day by typing in a few simple commands. More detail


ADVENTURE MODE

This mode is mainly used for Adventure maps and whatnot, but in general, it is survival mode, but restricted. You can only break certain blocks with your hands, some blocks other than Bedrock will be indestructible, and tools can only break certain blocks as well, assuming the have the appropriate tags.


SPECTATOR MODE

In spectator mode, you can fly through blocks, see what other entities are seeing by left-clicking on them, and you're completely invisible to everything except for other spectators. You can't interact with anything. When in third-person mode, you look like a translucent, floating head with no body. You can use the scroll wheel to adjust your flight speed, unlike flying in creative mode.


HARDCORE MODE

This is Survival mode, but locked at the hardest difficulty, and you cannot respawn. if you die, you are dead in this world and you have to delete it.


Tools and Weapons

Tools are the main components of Minecraft: they help you do everything you need to. They are some of the most important components of the game. Without a pickaxe, for example, you can't even collect anything like stone, Without a hoe, you can't farm, etc. These factors make tools some of the most important parts of a Minecraft world.


Pickaxe

The first tool you should ever build is a wooden pickaxe, as that way you can upgrade from wood tools to stone ones in your first day. The pickaxe is the only tool that lets you collect most blocks, like stone, ores, and cobblestone after being placed/found. you need ores because you will need to craft torches as soon as possible in your minecraft survival world (coal), as well as for upgrades on your other tools so you can craft more and new things, like buckets, and enchanting tables. More detail

Axe

The axe is a useful, but not necessary tool. The best time to get one is when you have a small base set up, with a stone sword and pickaxe at least, as well as a large stockpile of stone. The axe is used mainly to get wood faster, and wooden pickaxes are often considered useless, so start by getting a stone one first, but they are also not necessary whatsoever. All they do is make getting wood a bit easier and faster. More detail
